# Building Solutions from the Ground Up

Most people see Data Science as algorithms and numbers.
I see it as clarity in chaos-and direction when none exists.

For me, problem-solving is not just technical. It's personal. It comes from navigating uncertainty, adapting without a clear path, and still choosing to move forward.

## Where It Started

At 16, when higher education felt financially out of reach, I made a decision:

I wouldn't wait.

I started tutoring and freelancing-not because I had everything figured out, but because I didn't have another option. That phase shaped me early.

It taught me responsibility before comfort, discipline before motivation, and resilience before recognition.

I didn't have a safety net. So I built one.

## From Execution to Mastery

Over the next 7.5 years, freelancing became more than work-it became a way of thinking.

I delivered 30+ projects across operations, e-commerce, and automation, while completing my Software Engineering degree with a 3.81 CGPA.

Through that journey, I learned how to balance structure with adaptability-how to stay calm under pressure, and how to keep going even when things feel uncertain or unstructured.

Because real growth is rarely linear.

## The Pivot: Data as a Force Multiplier

At some point, I realized that solving problems wasn't enough-I wanted to solve them better, smarter, and at scale.

That's what led me to Berlin, where I'm now pursuing my Master's in Data Science at BHT.

Today, I focus on:

- Building AI-driven systems and automation
- Exploring Geospatial AI for real-world impact
- Applying statistical thinking to bring clarity and confidence into decisions

I don't just work with data-I use it to create direction.

## The Builder Behind the Work

What makes my journey different is not just what I've done-but how I experience and approach things.

I am someone who feels deeply and thinks independently.

I can be:

- Supportive and strong at the same time
- Direct, yet genuinely caring
- Soft in emotion, but firm in decisions

I naturally take responsibility-for my work, for people, and sometimes even more than I should. I hold high standards, both for myself and for others, because I believe growth requires honesty and effort.

At times, I can be complex-even a little contradictory. Calm yet ambitious. Sensitive yet bold. But that balance is what allows me to connect, adapt, and keep evolving.

And no matter what-
I stay determined to build something meaningful.

## Beyond Work

Beyond work, what drives me most is human impact.

I am deeply sensitive to struggle-especially when I see girls trying to build a future in environments where opportunities are limited and expectations are restrictive. Coming from a place where breaking norms is not easy, I understand that journey closely.

That's why I offer 1:1 guidance and support, helping students-especially girls-move forward in their education and careers. Not just with advice, but with belief, direction, and real conversations.

Because sometimes, what people need most is someone who understands-and doesn't let them give up.

I'm also someone who finds joy in exploring life beyond work:

- Cycling and hiking
- Traveling and experiencing different cultures
- Learning new languages (currently German B1/B2)

And quietly, I carry a deep love for animals.

I had a cat named Honey. She was part of my life for two years-more like family than a pet. Losing her this year left a space that still feels heavy. It reminded me how deeply I can care, and how important those small, meaningful connections really are.
