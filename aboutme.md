# Building Solutions from the Ground Up

Most people see Data Science as algorithms and numbers.
I see it as clarity in chaos-and direction when none exists.

For me, problem-solving is not just technical. It's personal. It comes from navigating uncertainty, adapting without a clear path, and still choosing to move forward.

<div class="about-snapshot">
  <span class="pill">7.5+ years execution journey</span>
  <span class="pill">30+ projects delivered</span>
  <span class="pill">3.81 CGPA Software Engineering</span>
  <span class="pill">M.Sc. Data Science at BHT Berlin</span>
</div>

<details class="story-block" open>
  <summary>Where It Started</summary>
  <p>At 16, when higher education felt financially out of reach, I made a decision: I wouldn't wait.</p>
  <p>I started tutoring and freelancing-not because I had everything figured out, but because I didn't have another option. That phase shaped me early.</p>
  <p>It taught me responsibility before comfort, discipline before motivation, and resilience before recognition.</p>
  <p>I didn't have a safety net. So I built one.</p>
</details>

<details class="story-block" open>
  <summary>From Execution to Mastery</summary>
  <p>Over the next 7.5 years, freelancing became more than work-it became a way of thinking.</p>
  <p>I delivered 30+ projects across operations, e-commerce, and automation, while completing my Software Engineering degree with a 3.81 CGPA.</p>
  <p>Through that journey, I learned how to balance structure with adaptability-how to stay calm under pressure, and how to keep going even when things feel uncertain or unstructured.</p>
  <p>Because real growth is rarely linear.</p>
</details>

<details class="story-block" open>
  <summary>The Pivot: Data as a Force Multiplier</summary>
  <p>At some point, I realized that solving problems wasn't enough-I wanted to solve them better, smarter, and at scale.</p>
  <p>That's what led me to Berlin, where I'm now pursuing my Master's in Data Science at BHT.</p>
  <p>Today, I focus on:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Building AI-driven systems and automation</li>
    <li>Exploring Geospatial AI for real-world impact</li>
    <li>Applying statistical thinking to bring clarity and confidence into decisions</li>
  </ul>
  <p>I don't just work with data-I use it to create direction.</p>
</details>

<details class="story-block" open>
  <summary>The Builder Behind the Work</summary>
  <p>What makes my journey different is not just what I've done-but how I experience and approach things.</p>
  <p>I am someone who feels deeply and thinks independently.</p>
  <p>I can be:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Supportive and strong at the same time</li>
    <li>Direct, yet genuinely caring</li>
    <li>Soft in emotion, but firm in decisions</li>
  </ul>
  <p>I naturally take responsibility-for my work, for people, and sometimes even more than I should. I hold high standards, both for myself and for others, because I believe growth requires honesty and effort.</p>
  <p>At times, I can be complex-even a little contradictory. Calm yet ambitious. Sensitive yet bold. But that balance is what allows me to connect, adapt, and keep evolving.</p>
  <p>And no matter what-I stay determined to build something meaningful.</p>
</details>

<details class="story-block" open>
  <summary>Beyond Work</summary>
  <p>Beyond work, what drives me most is human impact.</p>
  <p>I am deeply sensitive to struggle-especially when I see girls trying to build a future in environments where opportunities are limited and expectations are restrictive. Coming from a place where breaking norms is not easy, I understand that journey closely.</p>
  <p>That's why I offer 1:1 guidance and support, helping students-especially girls-move forward in their education and careers. Not just with advice, but with belief, direction, and real conversations.</p>
  <p>Because sometimes, what people need most is someone who understands-and doesn't let them give up.</p>
  <p>I'm also someone who finds joy in exploring life beyond work:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Cycling and hiking</li>
    <li>Traveling and experiencing different cultures</li>
    <li>Learning new languages (currently German B1/B2)</li>
  </ul>
  <p>And quietly, I carry a deep love for animals.</p>
  <p>I had a cat named Honey. She was part of my life for two years-more like family than a pet. Losing her this year left a space that still feels heavy. It reminded me how deeply I can care, and how important those small, meaningful connections really are.</p>
</details>
